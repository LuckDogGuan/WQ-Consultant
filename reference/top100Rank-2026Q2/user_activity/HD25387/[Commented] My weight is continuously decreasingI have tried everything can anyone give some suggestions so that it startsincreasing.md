### 评论 #36 (作者: CN44201, 时间: 1年前)

If your alpha’s performance (or weight) is decreasing, here are some possible reasons and solutions:

### **1. Check Recent Market Trends**

- Market conditions change, and an alpha that previously worked may no longer be effective.
- Test your alpha against recent data and see if it's still performing as expected.

### **2. Feature Engineering Adjustments**

- Your alpha might be overfitting to old data. Try adjusting your  **features**  (indicators, signals, factors) to be more robust.
- Introduce new  **market-neutral**  or  **risk-adjusted**  factors.

### **3. Optimize Hyperparameters**

- If you’re using machine learning, try tweaking your hyperparameters.
- Regularization techniques (L1/L2) can help reduce overfitting.

### **4. Improve Signal Stability**

- If your alpha is too volatile, its weight in the portfolio might decrease.
- Use  **smoother signals**  and avoid excessive sensitivity to market noise.

### **5. Diversification**

- A single alpha may not perform well across different assets.
- Combine multiple alphas to reduce dependency on one strategy.

### **6. Review Sharpe & IC Scores**

- Check your  **Sharpe ratio**  and  **Information Coefficient (IC)** .
  - A decreasing IC suggests your predictions are losing accuracy.
  - Improve it by refining factor definitions or adding new ones.

### **7. Avoid Data Snooping Bias**

- If your alpha is too closely fitted to historical data, it may not generalize well.
- Use  **out-of-sample testing**  and  **cross-validation**  to ensure robustness.
